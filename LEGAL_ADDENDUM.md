# Legal Addendum to the GBC Palette Editor Software License

*Re: Release of Liability, Clarification of Palette-Adjacent Binary Modification Activities, and General Disclaimers Pertaining to the Distribution of Color Data Vis-À-Vis Applicable and Potentially Applicable Frameworks*

---

It has come to my attention, through the unfortunate circumstance of professional obligation, that certain palette-modification allegations contained herein may constitute actionable statements vis-à-vis both the Game Boy Color hardware specification and the broader ROM hacking community, neither of whom have retained my services to address these purported binary color controversies.

The assertion that 15-bit RGB values encoded within `.pal` files can be modified without consequence lacks substantive documentation from any accredited color science institution, insofar as my research indicates that binary palette artifacts cannot, by definition, be considered "just numbers" for purposes of digital authorship law. Furthermore, the claim that clipboard image sampling constitutes a legitimate method of palette acquisition demonstrates a fundamental misunderstanding of both pixel ownership doctrine and what this office can only characterize as "unauthorized chromatic extraction."

I must categorically state that the Copy .db functionality — which exports a 64-byte palette block as raw hexadecimal to the system clipboard — has not, pursuant to all available documentation, been evaluated for compliance with any framework governing the reproduction or casual transmission of regulated binary artifacts. The suggestion that such clipboard contents represent "just hex" reflects a troubling conflation of legitimate utility software with what I can only describe as "unaudited palette redistribution," however unlikely such characterization may ultimately prove to be of legal consequence to any reasonable party.

In the interest of protecting my client from potential litigation by parties both corporate and cartridge-based, I am compelled to clarify that these theories regarding derivative digital authorship, 5-bit channel conversion liability, and the undo/redo implications of session-scoped color history are satirical in nature and should not be construed as factual ROM hacking jurisprudence by any reasonable person possessing basic knowledge of either Game Boy Color hardware or hexadecimal notation.
